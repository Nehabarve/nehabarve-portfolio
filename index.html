How I Think

My approach

Mental models shaped by real experience. Click to see examples.

Prioritization
Impact over urgency. The most visible feature isn't always the most valuable.
At Takeda, I chose SAP integration over UI polish — it unlocked end-to-end automation and delivered significantly more business value.
Collaboration
Good product work comes from shared understanding across teams.
On transparency reporting, I used real report samples to align vendor and business teams — concrete examples beat abstract requirements.
Conflict Resolution
Most conflicts are trade-offs that need structure, not a winner.
Business wanted speed, compliance wanted control. I designed tiered workflows based on risk — both sides got what mattered most.
Visibility First
You can't improve what you can't see. Measure before you optimize.
At Honeywell, teams knew things were "slow" but had no data. Cycle time dashboards gave them the first real view of bottlenecks.
Stakeholder Alignment
Ownership clarity drives coordination. Align early, reduce rework.
At Takeda, I ran working sessions with medical, compliance, and finance — making handoffs and ownership explicit for the first time.
Iterative Delivery
Start small, ship, learn. Action creates clarity that planning never will.
AI summarization at Honeywell started as a basic MVP. Each sprint brought real feedback. Clarity came from usage, not documents.
